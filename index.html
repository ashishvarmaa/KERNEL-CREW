User / Protagonist

The everyday traveler.

Millions of passengers on Indian Railways — families with children, elderly travelers, students, migrant workers, and long-distance commuters carrying substantial baggage for extended journeys.

— Their struggle

Where does the luggage go?

Bags blocking walkways, overcrowded seating, unsafe luggage placement, difficult movement around the coach, and a constantly higher risk of theft and damage to belongings.

— The quote

"Passengers book seats… but where does the luggage go?"

The question that started everything.

02
Context & Situation

The setting.

Indian Railways is one of the world's busiest networks, serving as the primary mode of long-distance transport for millions of people daily — yet its luggage infrastructure hasn't evolved with modern travel.

— 01

The current reality

Passengers carry substantial luggage for long trips. Designated under-seat storage quickly reaches capacity, forcing excess bags into aisles, doorways, and shared passenger spaces.

— 02

The missed opportunity

While floor space is heavily congested, significant unutilized vertical overhead space within coaches currently serves no functional purpose at all.

— 03

The network scale

From overnight sleepers to daytime intercity coaches — non-AC and seating-only coaches are where the luggage problem hits hardest, every single day.

03
Evidence Base

What the data tells us.

Field observations, passenger behavior studies, and proven design precedents from other transport sectors all point to the same conclusion.

— Finding 01

Passenger & luggage volume

The average long-distance passenger carries 1–2 large bags, consistently exceeding the capacity of traditional under-seat storage in standard coaches.

— Finding 02

Spatial audit

Visual analysis of standard Indian Railway coaches reveals a significant percentage of unutilized vertical clearance above head level — especially in non-AC and daytime seating coaches.

— Finding 03

Safety & mobility hazards

Observational data confirms aisle-blocking luggage is a primary cause of restricted mobility for passengers and catering staff, and poses a major tripping hazard.

— Finding 04

Material precedent

High-strength elastic mesh is already a proven, reliable storage mechanism — successfully utilized in automotive, aerospace, and bus transport to secure heavy loads in transit.

04
Pain Points & Needs

What passengers actually feel.

Through observation and empathy mapping we surfaced four core pain points — and three fundamental needs any solution must address.

User Pain Points
😣

Physical discomfort

Zero legroom due to large bags wedged between seats or underfoot.

🚶

Mobility anxiety

Constant stress of navigating blocked, hazardous aisles just to use the washroom.

🔒

Security concerns

Fear of theft when luggage must be left near coach doorways away from the passenger's seat.

💪

Lifting strain

Difficulty hoisting heavy bags into rigid, awkwardly positioned upper berths.

User Needs
— Need 01

Accessible security

Storage that is within the line of sight and close to the passenger's designated seat.

— Need 02

Unobstructed floors

A solution that completely removes luggage from the aisle and footwells.

— Need 03

Effortless organization

A flexible storage mechanism that adapts to different bag shapes without complex packing logic.

05
Root Causes · 5 Whys

Why does this persist?

Layered root-cause analysis — moving from surface symptoms down to the systemic reasons standard coaches still can't handle modern luggage.

WHY · 01

Outdated storage infrastructure

Standard coach designs have not evolved to accommodate modern travel behaviors. Under-seat dimensions were designed for smaller, softer bags rather than the large, hard-shell suitcases passengers use today.

WHY · 02

Systemic trust & brake-van avoidance

Passengers deliberately avoid checking bags into the train's separate luggage vans due to fears of theft, damage, or long retrieval delays. They prefer keeping belongings within eyesight.

WHY · 03

Architectural inefficiency

Traditional coach design focuses entirely on floor-level solutions (seating and under-seat space), leaving a massive volume of vertical air space near the ceiling completely unutilized.

WHY · 04

Economic travel patterns

Many long-haul passengers are students or migrant workers traveling for extended periods. This demographic carries heavy, oversized baggage that standard seating areas simply aren't equipped to handle.

06
How Might We…?

Reframing the challenge.

From a tangled problem space to three crisp, designable questions.

Core Design Challenge

How might we utilize the wasted vertical space in standard Indian Railway coaches to securely store heavy passenger luggage — so that aisles remain clear and passenger mobility is restored?

01

No structural modification

How might we create a secure overhead storage solution that requires absolutely no major structural changes to the existing coach?

02

Line-of-sight security

How might we ensure luggage stays within the passenger's line of sight to eliminate theft anxiety and the need for separate luggage vans?

03

Flexible & adaptive

How might we design a holding system flexible enough to accommodate the irregular shapes and large sizes of modern baggage?

07
Top Ideas

The solution stack.

Four interlocking design decisions that together form a complete, retrofittable luggage system for Indian Railway coaches.

Winning Concept
— Idea 01

The Elastic Overhead Mesh

Instead of rigid, heavy shelves, we suspend a high-strength flexible elastic net in the unused vertical space. It adapts to irregular luggage shapes while keeping the system extremely lightweight.

— Idea 02

Non-Invasive Mounting

A low-cost installation mechanism using metal hooks and screw fixtures that attach directly to existing coach walls. Zero major structural modifications to the train required.

— Idea 03

Anti-Slip & Sag Prevention

A reinforced plastic band at the front and a structural support wire ("Think Line") ensure the mesh doesn't sag under heavy weight and bags don't slip out during train movement.

— Idea 04

Line-of-Sight Security

Storage is placed directly above the passenger seating areas. Luggage stays constantly visible to its owner, dramatically reducing theft anxiety — no separate luggage van needed.

08
Prototype · Flow Diagram

How it works, step by step.

From installation to safe transit — the full passenger journey with the Elastic Overhead Mesh.

01

Retrofit installation

High-grade metal hooks are anchored to existing coach walls above seating areas — no structural changes to the train.

02

Passenger stowage

Upon boarding, passengers lift their large or irregularly shaped baggage into the flexible elastic mesh overhead.

03

Auto-securing lock

The reinforced front plastic band and anti-slip support wire tension around the luggage, locking it firmly in place.

04

Safe transit

Luggage stays secure and in direct line of sight. Aisles and footwells remain completely clear for the whole journey.
